Our Mission

What is Common Thread?

Acton-Boxborough has a long history of outstanding enrichment and community activities. These efforts emerge from individual schools, PTO groups, teachers, and volunteers — but they are rarely documented or shared district-wide.

The Common Thread initiative exists to change that. We are building a shared playbook of enrichment and engagement experiences so that any school, family, or volunteer can discover, organize, and build on what has worked before.

What We're Building

A six-month initiative

Phase 1 — Discovery

Cataloging existing enrichment and engagement activities across all AB elementary schools using a consistent documentation framework.

Phase 2 — Playbook

Creating step-by-step guides so any school, PTO, or volunteer can organize proven activities without reinventing the wheel.

Phase 3 — Community Events

Pilot community gatherings in summer 2025 to connect families, introduce the Task Force, and test programming ideas.

Phase 4 — Communications

Establishing a transparent hub-and-spoke communications framework with this site as the central source of truth.

Share an Activity

Help us document the enrichment and engagement activities that make AB special.

Do you know of an enrichment or engagement activity that has made a difference for students or families in Acton-Boxborough? We want to hear about it. Submissions are reviewed by the Common Thread Task Force and approved activities become part of the district-wide playbook.

What makes a good submission?

  • Activities that have actually been run in AB schools or with AB families
  • Enough detail that someone new could pick it up and run it
  • Honest notes about cost, logistics, and lessons learned
  • A valid email address so we can follow up if we have questions
Open the Submission Form →

The Process

What happens after you submit?

  1. You'll receive a confirmation email with a link to edit your submission at any time before it's reviewed.
  2. The Task Force will review your activity, usually within two weeks of submission.
  3. If we need more detail, we'll send you a follow-up email with specific questions.
  4. Once approved, your activity is formatted into a playbook one-pager and added to the catalog. You'll be notified by email.

About the Initiative

Who we are, what we're building, and how to get involved.

Our Purpose

Why Common Thread?

The Common Thread Task Force supports community connection and shared culture across Acton-Boxborough by identifying, organizing, and activating enrichment and engagement opportunities for elementary school families.

Acton-Boxborough already has a long history of successful enrichment and community activities. These efforts often emerge from individual schools, PTO groups, teachers, or volunteers, but they are rarely documented or shared district-wide. This initiative aims to preserve that legacy and make it accessible to everyone.

Deliverables

What we're building

📋 An Activity Catalog

A documented inventory of enrichment and engagement activities across all AB elementary schools, organized by category and school.

📖 A Shared Playbook

Step-by-step guides so any school, PTO, or volunteer can organize proven activities without starting from scratch.

🎉 Community Events

Two to three pilot community gatherings in summer 2025 to connect families and test programming ideas from the activity menu.

📇 A Contact Directory

A central directory of Task Force members, community partners, vendors, and district resources relevant to each activity.

Membership

Who we are

The Task Force includes representation from across the district community and meets bi-weekly during the first six months to maintain momentum.

Leadership

Chair, Co-Chair, and Administrative Sponsor provide direction and district coordination for the initiative.

Community Representatives

Parents, caregivers, teachers, staff, and community members representing all AB elementary schools.

School Committee

School Committee sponsorship ensures alignment with district goals and access to district resources.

Initiative Leads

Designated volunteer leads for specific activities or programs within the Task Force structure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about the Common Thread initiative.

Who can submit an activity?

Anyone connected to the Acton-Boxborough community — parents, caregivers, teachers, staff, administrators, or community members. You do not need a district Google account to submit.

What kinds of activities can I submit?

Any enrichment or engagement activity that has been run at or with AB schools — clubs, events, field trips, family programs, summer gatherings, and more. If it brought students or families together in a meaningful way, we want to know about it.

How long does review take?

The Task Force aims to review submissions within two weeks. You'll receive an email update when your submission is reviewed. If we need more information, we'll reach out directly.

Can I edit my submission after I send it?

Yes. Your confirmation email includes a link to edit your submission. You can update it at any time before it is approved.

What happens to approved activities?

Approved activities are added to the Activity Catalog on this site and formatted into a playbook one-pager — a Google Doc and PDF with a step-by-step guide so any school or volunteer can organize the activity in the future. You'll be notified by email when your submission is approved.

Is this connected to the School Committee?

The Common Thread Task Force operates under the Acton-Boxborough Regional School District with School Committee sponsorship. It includes representation from across the community including School Committee members, district administration, teachers, staff, and parents.

How can I get involved beyond submitting an activity?

Visit our About page to learn about volunteering, joining the Task Force as a community representative, or helping organize community events. The Task Force meets bi-weekly and welcomes new participants.

Where will the playbook be published?

The playbook lives on this site in the Activity Catalog. Each approved activity has its own page with a full how-to guide, downloadable as a PDF. The site is accessible to anyone with the link — no login required.

Have a question that's not answered here?

Include it in the "Anything else" field when you submit an activity — we read every submission.
